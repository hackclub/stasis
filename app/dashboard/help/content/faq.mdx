## General

### What is Stasis?

Stasis is a hardware hackathon by [Hack Club](https://hackclub.com) open to teenagers worldwide. Design and build original hardware projects, get funding to make them real, and earn bits along the way.

### When is the Stasis event?

The in-person Stasis event is May 15–18th, 2026!

### How do I get started?

Sign in to your Stasis account, create a new project from your dashboard, and choose a tier that fits your idea. If you're new to hardware, check out the [Starter Projects](/starter-projects) for guided builds. Work on your design, log your sessions, and submit for review when you're ready.

### What kind of projects can I make?

You can make anything related to hardware where you put the majority of your work into the hardware side of the project. This includes things like keyboards, 3D printers, and custom PCBs. Projects like home servers where you're mainly working on the software aspect are not allowed. If you're ever unsure, ask in #stasis-support on the Hack Club Slack.

### Where can I get help?

Ask in #stasis-support on the Hack Club Slack!

## Projects & Reviews

### How long may reviews take?

Reviews may vary in time — they can take a week or more. We try to review as soon as possible!

### How many projects can I make?

As many as you like! We just ask that you only do one of each starter project.

### What if my project gets rejected?

You'll receive feedback from the reviewer explaining what needs to be improved. Address the feedback and resubmit.

### Can I submit an existing project or one that I previously started?

If it's already finished, unfortunately not — start a new project. If you've just started or are in the middle of designing, you need to have journaled the entire process beforehand. If you're almost done designing, unfortunately not either.

### What tier is my project?

We can't give you an estimate until you design out your project. You can have a really complex idea, but if it is not polished, it will be a lower tier.

### Does my project need to have a PCB?

Not at all. Unless you're going advanced, most projects don't need PCBs, and we encourage hand-wiring components!

### Does Stasis allow group projects?

Yes! However, only one person can submit the project and access the journal.

### Can I use online tutorials?

Following a tutorial as a starting point is fine, but your submission needs a meaningful twist that's yours. See [Submission Guidelines](/docs/submission-guidelines) for details.

### Can we use tools like Ergogen?

You're free to use it to experiment with layouts and make design decisions, but the actual placement of footprints and the schematic has to be done manually by you.

### Can I use auto-routing software?

No. You must do your routing manually.

### Can I journal research?

Yes!

### Can I double dip with Summer of Making or any other Hack Club program?

No! Double-dipping is not allowed. If you have coding time for your project, you can journal it and get prizes from the tool bag.

## Funding & Parts

### How much money can I spend on my project?

You can spend up to the budget based on your quality tier, outlined in the [overview](/dashboard/help#overview). If you need more than that amount, you can use your own money. [Also see this about Hack Club and cost.](/docs/about-cost)

### Can I buy parts myself and get reimbursed later?

This depends on a case-by-case basis. Please ask in #stasis-support before making any purchases, as some things you buy may not get approved or your whole project may not be eligible.

### Can I get reimbursed for 3D printed parts?

Yep! We will partially reimburse you to the nearest 250g of filament.

### How will I get my 3D printed parts?

Through **Printing Legion!** It's an international network of Hack Clubbers 3D printing for each other. Check out [their website](https://printlegion.hackclub.com/).

### How do I top-up my grant?

If you need a top-up for your approved project, reach out in #stasis-support on the Hack Club Slack.

## Badges & Hackatime

### How are badges awarded?

Each project can earn up to 3 hardware achievement badges based on the techniques you used — I2C, SPI, WiFi, Bluetooth, CAD, Motors, Displays, Machine Learning, and more. Badges appear on your profile and project page.

### What is Hackatime?

Hackatime is a time-tracking tool used to log the hours you spend working on your project. Connecting it helps verify your build time.

### Are Hackatime-banned individuals allowed to participate?

Sorry, no!

## Other

It's hard to say without seeing your project, but generally speaking, as long as you follow the [submission guidelines](/docs/submission-guidelines) you're more than likely to be approved!
